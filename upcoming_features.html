Roadmap

Upcoming Features

Looking ahead, we plan to expand EscapeVRoom with several new features that strengthen both the gameplay experience and the project’s accessibility-focused message. Our upcoming goals include adding new puzzle mechanics, more realistic environmental barriers, and deeper wheelchair-based navigation systems.

  • Accessibility-related environmental barriers: We plan to introduce realistic frustrations such as doors that are slightly too narrow, hard-to-reach windows or ledges, small floor bumps, carpeted areas, and other design features that make movement more difficult.
  • Expanded wheelchair movement mechanics: We want to further develop how the wheelchair controls and feels in-game so that movement becomes a more meaningful part of the experience.
  • More realistic environmental frustrations and barriers.
  • Wheelchair upgrades: We are considering adding upgrades to the wheelchair that could help the player overcome certain obstacles, access new areas, or solve puzzles in different ways.
  • Vertical levels and multi-level puzzles: We hope to incorporate changes in elevation and multi-level spaces to create more complex gameplay while also emphasizing the added difficulty that vertical navigation can create for wheelchair users.
  • Visual cues and in-game hints to guide users
  • UI to prevent users from standing up
  • Music and in-game dialogues coming!
  • More levels!
