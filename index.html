Wheel-A-Way!

Wheelchair VR Escape Room

Sit in the seat of a wheelchair user and race to find Jack's passport before his flight.

EscapeVRoom is a VR escape-room experience set in Jack's neighborhood and home. You explore room-by-room, solve environment-driven puzzles, and navigate barriers from a wheelchair user's perspective to uncover the missing passport in time.

Narrative Escape Experience Accessibility-Centered Design VR Immersion

General Description

EscapeVRoom blends immersive puzzle design with accessibility-centered gameplay. Players are placed in a wheelchair-user perspective while moving through everyday spaces that become challenging when layouts are narrow, obstructed, or not thoughtfully designed.

The project originally explored a laser-tag facility concept, but the current narrative pivots to Jack's neighborhood and house to deliver a more grounded story objective: find the passport before departure.

Experience Flow

Setting

The game now takes place across Jack's neighborhood exterior and inside his house, using realistic room layouts and interactive props.

Gameplay

Players explore spaces, inspect clues, and solve interconnected puzzles that naturally progress toward the passport reveal.

Accessibility Theme

Out-of-reach objects, tight turns, awkward surfaces, and constrained spaces highlight how inaccessible design creates everyday frustration.

VR Immersion

VR makes movement, reach, and navigation feel immediate, making spatial decisions and barriers personally impactful.

Puzzle Design

Puzzles are interactive and meaningful, reinforcing the player's perspective while advancing the core narrative objective.

Movement & Interaction

Wheelchair-based movement and tool use are central. Players must maneuver carefully and problem-solve through physical constraints.

Our Mission

EscapeVRoom aims to build empathy and awareness by immersing players in the barriers wheelchair users can face, showing how inaccessible design can turn ordinary spaces into frustrating challenges.
